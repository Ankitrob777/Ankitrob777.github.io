Now, In order to understand whether our algorithm will take 2 clusters or 10, we use WCSS formula and graph.

Now, at first glance both name and formula of WCSS look a bit complex, but in reality it is super simple.
WCSS stands on Within Cluster Sum of Squares. It measure the squared average distance of all the points within a cluster
to the cluster centroid. In formula pi represent data points
of cluster and c1 ,c2,c3 are centroids. To calculate WCSS
,we have to first find Euclidean distance between a given point and the centroid to which it is assigned and square it.
Then we iterate this process for all points in cluster and at last sum all the squared distances.
